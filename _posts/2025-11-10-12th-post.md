# Week 12 – Database Designing and Professional Development

## Introduction
As the semester progressed, **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** introduced us to the practical aspects of database design. This stage was particularly important because designing a database correctly is the foundation of any successful software project.

## Database Designing Concepts
During the lectures, Dr. Bilal Ahmad taught us how to design databases professionally by identifying entities, attributes, and relationships. We learned that a well-designed database improves efficiency, reduces redundancy, and supports future scalability.

The major topics covered included:

- Entity Relationship (ER) Models
- Primary Keys and Foreign Keys
- Relationships between Tables
- Database Schema Design
- Data Integrity
- Relational Database Concepts

These concepts were demonstrated through practical examples that reflected real-world software systems.

## Digital Footprint Awareness
Apart from technical knowledge, **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** continuously encouraged students to maintain professional digital footprints. He emphasized the importance of documenting our learning journey through portfolios and GitHub repositories.

Following his guidance, I continued maintaining my portfolio to showcase my academic growth and practical learning experiences.

## Reflection
This week helped me understand that database design is much more than creating tables. It requires planning, analysis, and structured thinking. The professional insights provided by **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** made these concepts easier to understand and apply.
