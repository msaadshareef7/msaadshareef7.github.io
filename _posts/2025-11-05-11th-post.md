# Week 11 – Beginning of Second Semester and Introduction to Database Systems

## Introduction
The second semester started with new subjects including **Object Oriented Programming (OOP)**, **Database Systems**, **Digital Logic Design (DLD)**, **Calculus-II**, and **Statistics**. Among these subjects, Database Systems became one of the most interesting courses for me because it connects theoretical concepts with real-world applications.

## Learning Database Fundamentals
Our Database course is taught by **Dr. Bilal Ahmad**, whose guidance and teaching methodology helped us understand the importance of databases in modern software systems. During the initial weeks of the semester, he introduced us to the fundamentals of databases, their structure, purpose, and practical applications.

Dr. Bilal Ahmad explained how organizations manage large amounts of data efficiently through databases and how proper database design improves system performance and reliability. His lectures helped us understand the role of databases in software engineering and data-driven applications.

## Key Concepts Learned
During this week, we studied:

- Introduction to Database Systems
- Types of Databases
- Data Organization
- Database Components
- Importance of Data Management
- Real-world Applications of Databases

The concepts were explained through examples and practical discussions, making it easier to understand how databases are used in industry.

## Reflection
This week provided a strong foundation for learning databases. The guidance of **Dr. Bilal Ahmad** motivated us to explore database technologies beyond the classroom and understand their importance in software development and machine learning projects.
